Wax

There are many types of wax to use as a base for your candle projects. In order to reduce smoke and soot and keep with best practices, here is a list of the best wax types to use:

Soy Wax
Relatively cheap and most commonly used wax. Somewhat difficult for beginners to use.
Parasoy blends
This is probably the best type to start with as a beginner. It is easy to work with and relatively cheap to obtain.
Parrafin Wax
Another cheap and easily available wax. This one carries scent better than soy, but is more difficult for beginners.
Palm Wax
Another cheap and easily available wax. This one can be exceptionally difficult to work with so it is typically used in blends with other waxes.
Coconut blends
More expensive than others on our list, but this wax is one of the best carriers of scent and is fairly easy to work with.
Beeswax
This wax can be more expensive and tough for beginners. It also carries its own scent, so be mindful when adding other scents to your candle.

Health Concerns

Besides fire safety, we should be mindful of the ingredients we use in candles because we will breathe them in. One study in 2009 found that soy is far superior to paraffin wax in regards to health. Reports of cancer, asthma, and dermatitis have been reported in relation to use of paraffin candles.
