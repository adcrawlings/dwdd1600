Safe Practices

Candle making is a fun and rewarding hobby. They are beautiful decorations and add wonderful smells and ambiance to our homes. Many people enjoy using candles regularly and there are so many variations to match to your liking. However, there are inherent risks to keep in mind when it comes to candles and it is very important to follow best practices to minimize those risks. Some of these risks include (but are not limited to):

  • Risk of flame spreading
  • Risk of container breaking
  • Risk of contaminant inhalation and other injuries

Here at Safe Candle Making, we hope to be a resource for hobbyists like you to ensure best practices are used and these problems don't arise during your project.

Basic Fire Safety

Never leave a lit candle or hot wax warmer unattended. A tiny flame or electric spark can quickly become a large structure fire. If you are ever in a situation with a spreading fire, make sure to take these appropriate safety steps.

  1. Stay low to the ground to avoid heavy smoke.
  2. Evacuate your home. Before opening any doors, feel the door and knob to see if it is hot. If it is hot or smoke is coming from under the door, do not open it. Find another way out.
  3. Call 911 and answer all the dispatcher's questions

Use the Right Containers

The container you make your candle in should not be cracked, warped, or made out of flammable materials. It should be able to sit flat on a surface at least 12 inches away from any other items.
